Annual Quality Assurance Report (AQAR)

Academic Year: 2024–2025

STUDENT-CENTRIC DIGITAL MODULES (2024–25)

In the academic year 2024–25, the university has launched several new digital modules to enhance student experience, streamline administrative workflows, and promote transparency in grievance redressal and attendance management.

1. Student Grievance Module

A dedicated Student Grievance module has been integrated into both the University Management System (UMS) and the Subhartian mobile application. Through this feature, students can register formal complaints related to academic matters, college infrastructure, hostel facilities, or any other institutional issue. All grievances are tracked in real time with automated escalation protocols and resolution timelines.

2. Hostel Leave Module

The Hostel Leave process has been fully digitized via the Subhartian app. Students residing in university hostels can now apply for short-term or long-term leaves directly through their mobile devices. The request flows digitally to wardens and administrative authorities for approval, with automatic SMS/email notifications at every stage. This eliminates paperwork and ensures timely processing.

3. Digital Attendance Module

Attendance marking has transitioned to a fully digital system. Faculty members can now mark student attendance in real time using the Subhartian app under their Teaching role in the UMS portal. The system supports location-based validation, prevents proxy attendance, and instantly syncs data with academic records for internal assessment calculations.

ADMINISTRATION & STAFF MODULES (2024–25)

To empower teaching and non-teaching staff with seamless workplace support, the university has introduced advanced modules within the S-Touch and EMS platforms during 2024–25.

1. Complaint Management System

Staff members can now report infrastructure or facility-related issues—such as electricity failures, water supply disruptions, internet outages, or hardware malfunctions—through the S-Touch app or EMS portal. Each complaint is assigned a unique ticket number, routed to the relevant department (Civil, Electrical, IT, etc.), and tracked until resolution.

2. Staff Accommodation Module

Eligible teaching and non-teaching staff can apply for on-campus accommodation directly via S-Touch or EMS. The application includes document upload, preference selection, and real-time status tracking. Approvals are managed by the Estate Office with automated waiting list updates.

3. Research & Academic Performance (RAP) Module

The RAP module enables teaching faculty to submit evidence of research output—including journal publications, books, patents, or conference presentations—for incentive consideration as per university norms. Submissions are verified by the R&D Cell, and eligible incentives are processed through payroll integration. Applications are accepted via both S-Touch and EMS.

4. Gate Entry Facial Recognition System

A state-of-the-art facial recognition system has been deployed at all campus entry points. Authorized individuals—including students, staff, and family members of accommodated employees—are enrolled in the EMS biometric database. Entry is granted only after successful facial verification, enhancing campus security and eliminating unauthorized access.

INTEGRATION & INSTITUTIONAL IMPACT

All newly developed modules are natively integrated with the university’s core ERP, UMS, EMS, and Subhartian ecosystems. This ensures:

  • Single Sign-On (SSO) across platforms
  • Real-time data synchronization
  • Role-based access control
  • Audit trails for compliance
  • Reduced manual intervention and paper usage

These enhancements reflect the university’s commitment to digital governance, operational excellence, and stakeholder-centric service delivery in alignment with NAAC and NIRF benchmarks.
